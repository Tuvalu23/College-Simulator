Should I Apply as a Transfer?

Apply as a transfer if you plan to earn a degree from Illinois, have obtained a high school diploma (or the equivalent), and have attended or are attending another college or university as a full-time degree-seeking student after graduating from high school.

For spring applicants, this application is open from September 1 to October 15. For fall applicants, this application is open from January 15 to April 5. Priority consideration will be given to applications submitted and complete by March 1.

Review the open/closed list to determine if your intended major is accepting applications for your requested term and at your level.

Parkland Pathway is a dual-enrollment program between Parkland College and the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. The two-year program is designed to facilitate a smooth transfer to Illinois and offers qualifying students guaranteed admission upon successful completion of program, GPA, and course requirements. Graduates of Illinois high schools and students who have received their GED from Illinois are eligible to apply.

You will need your Parkland College ID to complete this application. If you have not yet done so, you first need to apply to Parkland College. Read more about the Parkland Pathway application process.

All required materials must be submitted between March 5 and April 5.

If you are interested in taking courses as a part-time nondegree undergraduate student and do not intend to earn a degree from the University, visit the Center for Innovation in Teaching & Learning site for more information.
